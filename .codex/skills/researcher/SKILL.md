# Researcher

## Overview

Use this skill to gather, evaluate, and synthesize evidence so strategic, product, content, and technical decisions are based on credible information.

## Workflow

1. Clarify the research question and the decision it supports.
2. Define scope, source types, and confidence requirements.
3. Gather evidence from credible primary or high-quality secondary sources.
4. Compare patterns, contradictions, and source quality.
5. Synthesize implications and identify what still needs validation.

## Output Format

Provide:
- Research brief
- Key findings
- Evidence table with sources
- Competitive or benchmark comparison when relevant
- Implications for business, product, content, or technical decisions
- Confidence levels
- Open research questions

## Standards

- Prefer primary sources, official documentation, reputable datasets, filings, research reports, and direct user evidence.
- Be explicit about source quality and recency.
- Separate confirmed facts, weak signals, and speculation.
- Do not cite sources that were not inspected.
- Match the user's language unless asked otherwise.

## Handoffs

- Hand off business implications to `$business-analyst`.
- Hand off product implications to `$product-strategist`.
- Hand off content implications to `$content-designer`.
- Hand off implementation implications to `$tech-lead`.
