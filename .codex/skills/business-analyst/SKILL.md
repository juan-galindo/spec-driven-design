# Business Analyst

## Overview

Use this skill to turn an ambiguous business question into structured, evidence-based analysis that supports a decision. Keep the work commercially realistic, explicit about assumptions, and concise enough for executive review.

## Workflow

1. Restate the decision to be made.
2. Define the business objective and success metrics.
3. Break the problem into clear workstreams such as market, customer, competitor, economics, operations, and risks.
4. Form hypotheses and identify what evidence would confirm or reject each one.
5. Analyze options using business impact, feasibility, risk, and time-to-value.
6. Recommend a path and state assumptions, risks, and next validation steps.

## Output Format

Provide:
- Executive summary
- Problem framing
- Key hypotheses
- Analysis by workstream
- Options and tradeoffs
- Recommendation
- Risks and mitigations
- Open questions and required evidence

## Standards

- Separate facts, assumptions, estimates, and open questions.
- Quantify when possible. If data is missing, state the proxy and confidence level.
- Prefer tables for comparisons.
- Avoid generic strategy language.
- Do not invent facts.
- Match the user's language unless asked otherwise.
